What is Poker?

Poker is a card game played by two or more people that is very common in casinos. It is considered the most popular card game in the world. It is class of games in which participants use cards in full or partial concealment to place bets for a central stack, after which the resulting bet is awarded to the player or players holding the best set of cards remaining at the end of the round, or to the remaining player if all others have folded. Because it involves game strategy, knowledge, decisions, among other factors, poker is considered a game of skill, unlike other games found in casinos (considered a game of chance).

Why play Poker?

There are a few reasons to play poker besides being a hobby or for fun. By playing often enough you consequently improve your powers of observation, as you have to pay attention to the other players' shares. It develops your patience, having to wait for the right moment to place a larger bet or when participating in long tournaments. It helps you to deal with losing situations, because as in all beginnings there will be failures and in them you will end up losing, but that's part of the game. It develops the habit of managing your resources, by knowing how much you are betting and how much you are getting back, and how to control your limits. And finally, it keeps your brain active, always thinking about the infinite possibilities.

Players

The most amazing thing about poker is its power of inclusion. Anyone can participate by studying and learning the basics of the rules and some essential knowledge. No matter your age, gender, or physical abilities, everyone can play as an equal, with the same chances to win.
